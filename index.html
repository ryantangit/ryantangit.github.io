Ryan Tan is a UC Berkeley alum pursuing an M.S. in Software Engineering at San Jose State University. He is currently a Software Engineering Intern at Institutional Shareholder Services' Innovation Lab, focusing on developing AI solutions for document analysis.

Early Life

Born in Guangdong Province, Ryan Tan spent most of his early childhood in China. He doesn't remember much but recalls some faint memories of memories of playing Rock, Paper, Scissors and tag with schoolmates. In the late 2000s, Ryan and his family immigrated to the state of California in the United States. He attended middle and high school in the Bay Area, where P.E. was his favorite class.

Adult Life and Education

Ryan attended the University of California, Berkeley in 2018 and graduated with a Computer Science undergraduate degree in 2021. During his freshman year in college, he was on the school's Hearthstone Esports team. It was around this time when he also became a naturalized citizen of the United States. In his sophomore year, the COVID-19 pandemic shut down the school campus, and he returned home to finish the remaining semesters of his college online. He considered CS61C (Computer Architecture) to be one of the more enjoyable classes at school. His favorite library was the air-conditioned Mainstack. His favorite campus bathroom was the one on Moffitt's fifth floor.

After completing his undergrad, Ryan moved to Salt Lake City, Utah to work as a software consultant for the State of Utah's DMV. His primary responsiblity was FastQ, the queuing system used in six major offices scattered across Utah. It was his first time in a long time being far away from the Bay Area and to his surprise, he really enjoyed Salt Lake City. While he was working, however, his mother had some health issues and he decided to move back where he can be closer with his family.

In 2024, he accepted an offer to enroll in San Jose State University as a Master in Software Engineering. He worked as an Instructional Teaching Assistant for a graduate Operating System course. He is still currently enrolled and researching artificial intelligence usage in academia for his master project. His favorite floor of King's Library is the sixth floor.

Alongside school, Ryan is also an intern at Internation Shareholder Services' Innovation Lab. His responsiblity mostly consists of developing internal AI-integrated tools for document analysis and data annotations.
