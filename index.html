Ryan Tan is a UC Berkeley alum pursuing an M.S. in Software Engineering at San Jose State University. He is currently a Software Engineering Intern at Institutional Shareholder Services' Innovation Lab, focusing on developing AI tools for document analysis and image annotations.

Early Life

Born in Guangdong Province, Ryan Tan spent most of his early childhood in China. He doesn't remember much, but recalls some faint memories of memories of playing Rock, Paper, Scissors and tag with schoolmates. In the late 2000s, Ryan and his family immigrated to the state of California in the United States. He attended middle and high school in the Bay Area, where P.E. was his favorite class.

Adult Life and Education

Ryan attended the University of California, Berkeley in 2018 and graduated with a Computer Science undergraduate degree in 2021. During his freshman year, he was on the school's Hearthstone Esports team. It was around this time that he also became a naturalized citizen of the United States. In his sophomore year, the COVID-19 pandemic shut down the campus, and he returned home to finish his remaining college semesters online. He considered CS61C (Computer Architecture) to be one of his more enjoyable classes. His favorite library was the air-conditioned Mainstack, and his favorite campus bathroom was the one on Moffitt's fifth floor.

After completing his undergraduate degree, Ryan moved to Salt Lake City, Utah, to work as a software consultant for the State of Utah's DMV. His primary responsibility was FastQ, the queuing system used in six major offices scattered across Utah. This was his first time in a long time being far away from the Bay Area, and to his surprise, he really enjoyed Salt Lake City. However, while he was working, his mother experienced some health issues, and he decided to move back to be closer to his family.

In 2024, Ryan accepted an offer to enroll in San Jose State University as a Master in Software Engineering student. He also worked as an Instructional Teaching Assistant for a graduate Operating System course. He's currently still enrolled and researching the use of artificial intelligence in academia for his master's project. His favorite floor of King's Library is the sixth floor.

During the summer of 2025, Ryan was also an intern at Internation Shareholder Services' Innovation Lab in Menlo Park. His main responsiblity was developing AI-powered tools for document analysis and data annotations.

Currently, he is now back in school to working on his Master's Project with a focus on Agentic AI.
